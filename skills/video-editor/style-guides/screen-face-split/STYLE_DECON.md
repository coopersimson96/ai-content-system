# Screen-Face Split Motion Style — Deconstruction

> Reference set: 4 reels — `DXh3_WRkrod` (134s, Airtable+GitHub demo), `DW8sYdqiPTR` (216s, agentic-AI tutorial), `DXauOnRNn_k` (92s, Carpathia agentic OS), `DXe1B6Ij5hH` (47s, Lindy AI ad)
> Format: 9:16 vertical, 1080×1920, 30fps
> Genre: Talking-head + screen-recording / headline-card hybrid; AI/tech ad + tutorial register
> Working alias for this aesthetic: **Screen-Face Split** — hard horizontal split, top-half is the "what," bottom-half is the "who"

This is a deconstruction of the most prevalent archetype in our reference saves into a reusable motion-graphics playbook. The goal is *not* one-to-one recreation — it is to extract the **visual DNA** so it can be applied to your scripts via Hyperframes without copying any single source.

This is the **third archetype** in the style-guide library (siblings: `sage-stack`, `editorial-magazine`). It is the most workmanlike and the most copyable for ad-style content.

---

## 0 · The 10 Laws (memorize)

1. **The bottom half never moves; only the top half changes. The speaker is the spine.** A hard horizontal cut at 50% (sometimes 55/45) divides every frame. The bottom half is one continuous talking-head clip that runs the entire reel. The top half flips between scenes — screen recordings, headline cards, dashboards, flowcharts — but the speaker's face never breaks. This is the single biggest move.
2. **Caption emphasis is one word per beat, in a brand-tied color, NEVER more than one word colored at once.** White word + colored word is the signature. The colored word is the "anchor" of that phrase — the noun, the verb, the number, the brand name. Two colored words in the same caption breaks the rhythm. One word, one color, every time.
3. **The emphasis color is the brand color of whatever's being discussed in the top half.** Lindy = gold `#d4a02b`. Krause's stack = cyan `#37bdf8`. Carpathia's Agentic OS = hot red `#ef3b3b`. The caption color and the top-half graphic are color-locked.
4. **Naturally-graded speaker. Do not desaturate.** Unlike sage-stack (which strips color from the face), this archetype celebrates the speaker's real environment — neon backlighting, plants, kitchen, bookshelves, studio gear, all visible and naturally colored. The speaker is the protagonist, not a B-roll element. Their authenticity is the trust device.
5. **Hard cuts, not whip-blurs.** The top half changes via hard cuts (or the occasional 80ms cross-dissolve). No motion blur, no chromatic aberration, no `whip-pan`. This archetype is workmanlike — it earns trust through speed and substance, not through cinematography. Compare: sage-stack hides every cut inside a blur; screen-face split shows you the cut.
6. **Captions live in the speaker half ONLY, mid-torso.** Captions sit on the speaker's chest area, roughly y=65-75% of the full frame. They never appear on the top-half graphic. The top half is for showing; the bottom half is for reading.
7. **Word-by-word reveal, 1-3 words at a time. Never karaoke phrase-blocks.** The caption track ticks forward at the pace of the speaker's syllables. Each tick is a fresh 1-3 word group, NOT a long highlighted phrase. ~0.4-0.8s per group.
8. **Bold sans-serif captions, all-caps OR title-case, heavy weight.** Inter Black / Geist Bold / SF Pro Display Heavy. NOT Montserrat (too round). NOT a serif. The captions read as broadcast-news subtitles, not editorial typography.
9. **The mic is part of the composition.** A Shure SM7B or similar broadcast mic is visible in the bottom half on every reel. It anchors the "podcast / authority" register. Never crop it out.
10. **The top half rotates between three modes per video: Screen Recording, Headline Card, Custom Dashboard.** Mode A = real product UI with cursor visible. Mode B = bold sans headline on flat color. Mode C = custom-rendered dashboard / flowchart / metric card. A single video uses ALL THREE — switching modes is the visual variation that keeps a long talking head alive.

## 1 · The Reference Timeline (DXe1B6Ij5hH, 47s, beat-by-beat)

The Lindy AI ad is the cleanest example. It runs 47s, fast-paced, mid-funnel ad register. The other three reels follow the same grammar but stretched longer for tutorials.

| Time | Beat | Top-half mode | What's on screen | Caption (color emphasis) |
|------|------|---------------|------------------|---------------------------|

**Three-act structure** (compressed for ad register):
- **Hook (0-6s):** Headline card promises the outcome. Speaker establishes presence.
- **Demonstration (6-38s):** Three use-cases, each ~8s. Each = one top-half mode change + one caption with one colored word.
- **CTA (38-47s):** Site URL → comment-bait close. Speaker fills the frame for the engagement ask.

Notice: the top half rotates between modes A, B, C through the video. Variety in the top half is what keeps a 47s ad from feeling like a single static screen. The bottom half is **utterly continuous.**

### Notes on the other three reels

- **DXh3_WRkrod (134s, Airtable + GitHub):** Long-form tutorial register. Top half shows real Airtable Grid View and a real GitHub repo page — NO custom dashboards. Caption emphasis color is **cyan** because Krause's accent. Speaker has dramatic neon-blue/purple backlight bar.
- **DW8sYdqiPTR (216s, agentic-AI tutorial):** Even longer. Heavy use of Mode C (custom-rendered dashboards). Captions are ALL-CAPS bold sans, **cyan** emphasis (`#3CD9C5`). Pill-style backdrop on captions. Closest to a "video essay" register.
- **DXauOnRNn_k (92s, Carpathia Agentic OS):** Hybrid ad/tutorial. Top half is mostly Mode C (custom Agentic OS dashboard with orange line-chart, dark glass UI), with brief Mode A cuts to an Obsidian canvas. Caption emphasis is **hot red** (`#ef3b3b`). Distinctive: in some beats the top half drops away entirely and the speaker fills the full 9:16, giving the close a "look me in the eye" moment.

The grammar is identical across all four. What changes: which top-half mode dominates, and which brand-tied accent color the captions use.

## 2 · The Visual Vocabulary

### 2.1 The split (the load-bearing layout)

| Element | Spec |
|---------|------|
| **Top half** | `position: absolute; top: 0; left: 0; right: 0; height: 50%;` (sometimes 52% or 55%) |
| **Bottom half** | `position: absolute; bottom: 0; left: 0; right: 0; height: 50%;` |
| **Gap** | None most of the time. Occasionally a 0-24px solid black gutter. Treat the gap as optional. |
| **Top-half background fill** | When the screen recording / card doesn't fill the entire top half, the leftover space is **pure black `#000`**. |
| **Aspect of the inner content** | Top-half content is usually a desktop screen recording at native 16:9 or 16:10, letterboxed inside the top half on black. Sometimes a subtle drop-shadow + rounded corners (`border-radius: 18px; box-shadow: 0 24px 48px rgba(0,0,0,.4);`) to feel like a floating "device." |
| **Speaker crop** | Bottom half is a 9:8-ish crop of the speaker — chest-up, head occupies upper 60% of the bottom half, mic visible bottom-center. |

### 2.2 Top-half content modes

#### Mode A — Screen Recording
- Real product UI captured natively. Common targets: Airtable, GitHub, Cursor, Claude Code, Notion, Lindy, ChatGPT, Obsidian.
- Real cursor visible (sometimes magnified — DXh3 has a custom large white cursor for legibility on phone screens).
- Often dark-mode UI; if light-mode, the surrounding letterbox stays black.
- Very small text is the trade-off: zoom into the relevant region rather than show the full app.
- No artificial frame / monitor mockup needed — just the OS chrome (red/yellow/green dots in the corner is enough signal).

#### Mode B — Headline Card
- Bold sans-serif headline on flat color background.
- **Two-color split typography:** one word/clause in a dark color (navy `#0d1f3c`, near-black, or forest), one word/clause in an accent (gold `#d4a02b`, cyan, or warm orange).
- Small-caps subtitle above the headline, smaller, in the accent color: `LINDY AI AGENTS`, `KRAUSE'S META ADS STACK`, etc. Letter-spacing wide (`0.18em`).
- Background: cream `#f7f1de` (warm) OR off-white `#f0eeea` OR pure white.
- **Subtle drop shadow on the headline:** `text-shadow: 0 2px 0 rgba(0,0,0,.10), 0 6px 12px rgba(0,0,0,.08);`
- One headline = one beat. Hold for ~3-5 seconds, then cut.

#### Mode C — Custom Dashboard / Diagram
- Custom-rendered UI mock that looks like an actual product but is built in HTML/CSS for the video.
- Common shapes: line chart with one accent stroke, flowchart with rounded-rect nodes connected by lines, message bubble + LinkedIn-icon panel, agentic-system architecture diagram.
- Typography inside: monospace for technical (`UI Mono` per type system below) OR bold sans for headers.
- Very dark background `#0a0e14` with thin colored accent line (orange `#ff6b35` or teal `#3CD9C5`) is a recurring recipe.
- Often has a subtle gradient glow behind it (radial cyan/teal halo on a darker plate) to make it pop.

### 2.3 Type System

**Three roles, two families.**

#### Voice A — "Caption" (heavy sans-serif, white + one accent word per beat)
- Family: **Inter Black**, **Geist Black**, **SF Pro Display Heavy**, or **Helvetica Now Black**.
- Weight: 900 (Black). Anything lighter than 800 reads weak at 9:16.
- Color: `#ffffff` for non-emphasis words. Brand-tied accent for emphasis word (see §2.4).
- Letter-spacing: `-0.01em` to `-0.02em` (tight).
- Line-height: `1.05`-`1.15`.
- Text-shadow: `0 4px 8px rgba(0,0,0,.6)` for readability over the speaker.
- Caption variants observed:
  - **Plain on transparent** — text floats on the speaker with shadow only
  - **Pill** — `background: rgba(0,0,0,.78); border-radius: 999px; padding: 12px 28px;`
  - **Stroke** — `-webkit-text-stroke: 6px black;` heavy black outline so it pops on light AND dark backgrounds
- Size: ~64-96px on a 1080×1920 canvas (3.5-5.0% of width).

#### Voice B — "Headline" (display sans, two-color split)
- Family: a display sans with strong weight contrast — **DM Sans Bold/Black**, **Cabinet Grotesk Black**, **General Sans Bold**, or stay with **Inter Black** for unity.
- Color split: one color near-black (`#0d1f3c` navy or `#1a1a1a` ink), one color brand-accent (gold/cyan/orange).
- Size: 80-120px on the 1080-wide top half.
- Used in Mode B headline cards only.
- Always paired with a small-caps subtitle in the accent color, e.g. `LINDY AI AGENTS` at 28-34px, letter-spacing 0.18em.

#### Voice C — "UI Mono" (in-product overlays only)
- Family: **JetBrains Mono**, **Geist Mono**, **SF Mono**.
- Color: dark gray on light surfaces, light teal/orange on dark surfaces.
- Used inside Mode C dashboards (axis labels like `MAR 23 / MAR 30`, integration tag list `GITHUB:GITHUB · GMAIL · GOOGLE DRIVE`).
- Never used for spoken-word captions.

### 2.4 Color Story — The Caption Accent Color Is a Brand Decision

| Color | Hex | Meaning | When to use |
|-------|-----|---------|-------------|
| **Cyan / teal** | `#37bdf8` / `#3CD9C5` | Default tech-credibility accent | Default for screen-rec heavy beats; AI/dev tooling brand register; "smart, technical" energy |
| **Hot red** | `#ef3b3b` | Urgency, attention, "look here" | Use sparingly — feels louder than cyan. Good for "premium / urgency / call-out" beats |
| **Gold / yellow** | `#d4a02b` / `#f5c842` | Premium, ad-register, money-on-the-line | Lindy ad. Pairs with navy. Use when the script literally talks about money saved, ROI, premium value |
| **Hot orange** | `#f09025` | Energy, action, "do this now" | Lower-saturation than red, warmer than gold |
| **Navy / ink** | `#0d1f3c` / `#1a1a1a` | Anchor color in headline cards | Always paired with a brighter accent in Mode B headlines |
| **Cream paper** | `#f7f1de` | Warm Mode B background | Lindy-style ad cards. Avoids harsh white |
| **Black void** | `#000` | Top-half letterbox / empty space | The default fallback fill |
| **Dashboard charcoal** | `#0a0e14` / `#0e1117` | Mode C dashboard background | Carpathia Agentic OS, Cursor IDE captures |

**Discipline:** ONE accent color per video. You do not switch the caption emphasis color mid-reel. The accent is a brand promise — it's how the viewer knows whose video they're watching after 30 seconds.

**Selection rule (when writing scripts):** look at the dominant top-half graphic and pick the caption accent that matches its strongest brand color. If the script discusses one-brand tooling for the whole reel, captions stay one accent. If a sponsored beat shows another brand's UI, that beat's caption accent can match — but only that beat.

## 3 · Signature Element Recipes

### 3.1 The Hard Split (load-bearing)

```html
<div class="reel" data-composition-id="screen-face-split"
     data-start="0" data-width="1080" data-height="1920">

  <!-- Bottom half: speaker (the spine — runs entire reel) -->
  <div class="speaker-half" data-start="0" data-duration="47" data-track-index="0">
    <video src="assets/speaker.mp4" muted></video>
  </div>

  <!-- Top half: rotates between scenes -->
  <div class="top-half-stage" data-start="0" data-duration="47" data-track-index="1">
    <!-- Sub-comp clips swap here; each clip occupies the full top-half frame -->
  </div>

  <!-- Captions: live in bottom-half region only -->
  <div class="captions-track" data-start="0" data-duration="47" data-track-index="2"></div>
</div>
```

```css
.reel { position: relative; width: 1080px; height: 1920px; background: #000; overflow: hidden; }

.top-half-stage {
  position: absolute; top: 0; left: 0; right: 0;
  height: 50%;                /* ← the split */
  background: #000;            /* default letterbox */
  overflow: hidden;
}

.speaker-half {
  position: absolute; bottom: 0; left: 0; right: 0;
  height: 50%;                 /* ← matches the top */
  overflow: hidden;
}
.speaker-half video {
  width: 100%; height: 100%; object-fit: cover; object-position: center 30%;
  /* NO filter. Naturally graded. This is the whole point. */
}
```

Optional: add a 4-8px black gutter at the split line for a more "designed" feel:

```css
.reel::before {
  content: ''; position: absolute; left: 0; right: 0; top: calc(50% - 2px);
  height: 4px; background: #000; z-index: 10;
}
```

### 3.2 Caption Emphasis System (THE signature move)

The signature is one word per caption group rendered in the brand accent color. Build it as word-level reveal where each `<span>` has its own start/duration AND optional emphasis class.

```html
<div class="caption" data-start="6.0" data-duration="0.8">
  <span class="word" data-start="6.0" data-duration="0.4">automates</span>
  <span class="word" data-start="6.4" data-duration="0.2">the</span>
  <span class="word emphasis" data-start="6.6" data-duration="0.5">boring</span>
</div>
```

```css
.caption {
  position: absolute;
  top: 65%;                    /* sits in the speaker's chest area */
  left: 50%;
  transform: translateX(-50%);
  font-family: 'Inter', system-ui, sans-serif;
  font-weight: 900;
  font-size: 84px;
  letter-spacing: -0.015em;
  line-height: 1.1;
  color: #ffffff;
  text-align: center;
  text-shadow: 0 4px 12px rgba(0,0,0,.65);
  white-space: nowrap;
}

.word { display: inline-block; opacity: 0; }
.word.emphasis { color: var(--accent, #6a9e88); }   /* the brand accent */

/* Pill variant */
.caption.pill {
  background: rgba(20,20,20,.82);
  padding: 14px 32px;
  border-radius: 999px;
  text-shadow: none;
}

/* Stroke variant */
.caption.stroke .word {
  -webkit-text-stroke: 6px #000;
  paint-order: stroke fill;
}
```

```js
// Reveal each word at its data-start
gsap.utils.toArray('.caption .word').forEach((el) => {
  const start = parseFloat(el.dataset.start);
  tl.to(el, { opacity: 1, duration: 0.05, ease: 'none' }, start);
});

// Punch-in on the emphasis word — a 70ms scale pulse the moment it appears
gsap.utils.toArray('.caption .word.emphasis').forEach((el) => {
  const start = parseFloat(el.dataset.start);
  tl.fromTo(el,
    { scale: 1.18 },
    { scale: 1.0, duration: 0.18, ease: 'power3.out' },
    start
  );
});
```

**Important rules:**
- Only ONE word per caption group gets `.emphasis`. Two emphasis words = visual chaos.
- The emphasis word is usually the noun OR verb (rarely the modifier).
- The accent color is set as a CSS custom property at the reel level so it can be brand-swapped in one place: `style="--accent: #6a9e88;"` on `.reel`.
- A subtle 0.18s scale pulse (1.18 → 1.0) on the emphasis word the instant it appears is the secret sauce. It doesn't read as animation, it reads as *emphasis*.

```js
// Single entry: kicker fades up, then headline words ladder in
gsap.timeline()
  .from('.kicker', { y: 16, opacity: 0, duration: 0.4, ease: 'power3.out' })
  .from('.headline span', {
    y: 36, opacity: 0, duration: 0.45,
    ease: 'expo.out', stagger: 0.07
  }, '-=0.15');
```

That's the whole entry. No flourish, no orbit, no continuous idle. Land it, hold it, cut.

## 4 · Motion Patterns

### 4.1 Transition library

| Transition | When to use | Implementation |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| **Hard cut** | Default for top-half mode changes (~95% of cuts) | Just swap visibility on the timeline. `gsap.set(prevScene, { autoAlpha: 0 })` and `gsap.set(nextScene, { autoAlpha: 1 })` at the cut frame. |
| **80ms cross-fade** | Soft transition between two related top-half scenes (e.g. scrolling Airtable → zoomed Airtable detail) | `tl.to(prev, { autoAlpha: 0, duration: 0.08 }, t).to(next, { autoAlpha: 1, duration: 0.08 }, t);` |
| **Quick zoom-push** | Inside Mode A screen-rec for "look here" emphasis | `gsap.to(scene, { scale: 1.15, duration: 0.4, ease: 'expo.out' });` paired with the cursor moving to the same spot |
| **Top-half drop (full-screen speaker)** | The CTA close — top half slides up off-screen, speaker video scales to fill 100% | `gsap.to('.top-half-stage', { y: '-100%', duration: 0.4, ease: 'power3.in' })` + `gsap.to('.speaker-half', { height: '100%', duration: 0.4, ease: 'power3.in' })` synchronized |
| **Caption word reveal** | Every spoken word | Per-word opacity tween (see §3.2) |
| **Emphasis pulse** | The signature scale-pulse on accent words | `fromTo(el, {scale: 1.18}, {scale: 1.0, duration: 0.18, ease: 'power3.out'})` |

**No whip-pan. No glitch. No light-leak. No chromatic aberration.** Those tools belong to other archetypes (sage-stack uses them everywhere). This archetype sells substance through speed and cleanliness — visual flourish would betray the "I'm just showing you what works" promise.

### 4.2 Easing rules

- Caption word reveals: **`none`** (snap on) — captions are diegetic, not animated
- Emphasis pulse: **`power3.out`** — fast settle
- Headline-card entry: **`expo.out`** with 0.07s stagger — confident, not bouncy
- Top-half mode swaps: **none** (hard cut) or **`none`** linear 80ms cross-fade
- Speaker half: **never animated** — the spine doesn't move
- Full-screen speaker close: **`power3.in`** going up, **`power3.out`** if reversing

Never use `bounce`, `elastic`, `back`, or `circ`. Those would break the workmanlike register.

### 4.3 Pacing target

| Reel length | Avg beat | Beats total |
|-------------|----------|-------------|
| 30-50s ad | 3-5s per beat | 8-12 beats |
| 60-120s tutorial | 4-8s per beat | 12-20 beats |
| 120s+ deep tutorial | 5-10s per beat | 18-30 beats |

The bottom-half speaker is one continuous take. The top-half cuts at 3-10s intervals. Caption words tick at 0.4-0.8s per group (matches normal speech pacing).

## 5 · The Speaker Treatment (CRITICAL — this is half the style)

### 5.1 The grading (or rather: the lack of it)

```css
.speaker-half video {
  /* No filter. */
  /* The whole point of this archetype is the speaker is naturally graded. */
}
```

That's it. **No `saturate()`, no `sepia()`, no vignette, no cool tint.** If you find yourself reaching for `filter:` on the speaker, stop — you're heading toward sage-stack territory.

The ONLY post-production a screen-face-split speaker should get:
- Light color correction in the source clip itself (white balance, exposure)
- Mild sharpening (`filter: contrast(1.04)` is the absolute maximum, and only if the source is soft)

### 5.2 Framing rules

- **Crop:** chest-up, head occupies upper 60% of the bottom half
- **Eyeline:** locked center, looking into the camera
- **Position:** centered horizontally
- **Mic:** visible bottom-center or bottom-right. Broadcast mic preferred (Shure SM7B / SM57 / RØDE PodMic).
- **Background:** the speaker's REAL setup. Visible. Naturally lit. Plants, bookshelves, neon backlight bars, kitchen, studio.
- **Lighting:** can be dramatic (DXh3's neon-blue/purple bar lighting), studio-flat (DXe1's plain wall), or natural-warm (DXau's home office). Any of these reads correctly. The constant is "real."
- **Audio:** broadcast-quality. The mic is in frame because the audio is real.

### 5.3 Why naturally-graded matters

This archetype's trust device is *authenticity, not aesthetics*. Sage-stack desaturates the face because the graphics are doing the heavy lifting and the face needs to recede. Screen-face split inverts that: the face IS the heavy lifting, and the graphic is just "showing what they're talking about." Desaturating the face here would betray the format — suddenly the speaker becomes a B-roll element, and the viewer loses the protagonist.

## 6 · The Repeatable Beat Grammar (the format)

Every beat in a screen-face-split reel uses the same internal structure:

```
[ Top-half mode ] (A/B/C)         ~3–8s
   + [ Caption ] (1 emphasis word) ~0.4–0.8s per word group
   + [ Speaker continuous ]        (entire reel)
```

A beat is `(top-half visual asset) × (one or more caption groups) × (continuous speaker)`. Beats are structurally identical; what changes is which top-half mode and which sentence the speaker says.

### Beat A — Mode A (Screen Recording)
- Real product UI fills the top half
- Cursor visible, ideally moving through the relevant action
- Caption: 2-4 word group with one emphasis word in the brand accent
- Audio: speaker says "I use [tool] to [job]" or "look at this — [thing happens]"
- Duration: 4-8s typical

### Beat B — Mode B (Headline Card)
- Bold sans headline + small-caps subtitle on cream/off-white
- Two-color split typography (ink + accent)
- Caption: shorter (1-2 words), often no color emphasis (the headline is the emphasis)
- Audio: speaker says the punchline / promise / number
- Duration: 3-5s — these read instantly

### Beat C — Mode C (Custom Dashboard)
- HTML/CSS-rendered fake UI showing a flow, chart, message, or system diagram
- Subtle accent-color glow behind it
- Caption: 2-4 words, one emphasis
- Audio: speaker explains *what the system does* in one sentence
- Duration: 5-10s — Mode C beats are the slowest because they require comprehension

### Beat D — Full-Screen Speaker (CTA / climax only)
- Top half drops away, speaker fills 100% of frame
- Tight head-and-shoulders crop
- Caption: short, no emphasis (the face is the emphasis)
- Audio: the close — "comment X," "go to URL," "trust me on this"
- Duration: 1.5-3s; happens once per reel, at the end

### Transition between beats
- Hard cut on the top half
- Speaker continues unbroken
- Captions tick forward independently of top-half cuts

This grammar makes the format infinitely scalable: any list-style or demonstration script maps to a sequence of A/B/C beats with one D beat at the end.

### 7.2 Word-level caption recipe (the high-leverage template)

This is what powers the signature one-word-color-emphasis system. Use a transcript JSON (from `npx hyperframes transcribe`) and build per-word `<span>` elements with `data-start` and `data-duration`. Mark the emphasis word with `class="emphasis"`.

```js
// Build caption DOM from a transcript JSON
function buildCaptions(transcript, emphasisIndex) {
  // transcript: [{ word, start, end }]
  // emphasisIndex: which word index in this group to emphasize
  return transcript.map((w, i) => `
    <span class="word ${i === emphasisIndex ? 'emphasis' : ''}"
          data-start="${w.start}"
          data-duration="${w.end - w.start}">${w.word}</span>
  `).join(' ');
}
```

The "which word to emphasize" decision can be heuristic:
- Pick the longest word in the group
- OR pick the noun/verb (use a simple POS tagger or hand-author)
- OR pick the brand name / number / metric if present

### 7.3 Mode B headline card recipe

See §3.3 above. Wrap in a Hyperframes sub-composition file `compositions/v01-headline.html` with its own `data-composition-id` and a paused timeline registered to `window.__timelines`.

### 7.4 Mode C dashboard recipe

See §3.4 above. Use the **`data-chart`** registry block as a starting point and override its colors. Or build custom — the dashboard is unique to each script anyway.

### 7.5 Registry block mappings

When wiring real Hyperframes blocks to this style:
- **`app-showcase`** → use for Mode A when you want a tilted/floating phone-app frame around a screen recording. Default flat-letterbox is also fine (and more on-archetype).
- **`data-chart`** → use for Mode C dashboards. Override stroke color to match brand accent.
- **`flowchart`** → use for Mode C agentic-system diagrams.
- **`grain-overlay`** → 2.5% opacity, always on. Subtle film bias.
- **`logo-outro`** → adapt for the comment-CTA full-screen speaker close.
- **DO NOT use:** `whip-pan`, `light-leak`, `glitch`, `chromatic-radial-split`, `cinematic-zoom`. These belong to other archetypes.

### 7.6 Render contract reminders

- Speaker `<video>` must be `muted` if you have a sibling audio track. If the speaker mp4 has embedded audio, use `data-has-audio="true"` and remove `muted` (see `render-contract-gotchas.md`).
- Don't add `class="clip"` to `<video>`.
- Wrap `<video>` in a `<div>` if you ever animate scale (e.g. the full-screen speaker close — animate the wrapper's `height: 50%` → `100%`, not the video's height).
- Captions as a single `.captions-track` clip with word `<span>`s inside is cleaner than one clip per word.

## 8 · Brand Adaptation

See **[`../BRAND.md.example`](../BRAND.md.example)**. The format and grammar above stays as documented; all colors and type voices defer to your `BRAND.md`.

### Archetype-specific overrides

This archetype's format requires a few things that override BRAND.md's defaults:

- **Caption type is bold sans, NOT italic-serif.** BRAND.md may prescribe italic-serif captions as the brand default. Screen-face-split overrides this — the format's identity is **bold sans (Inter Black / Geist Black, ≥800 weight) with single-word color emphasis**. Italic-serif captions would collapse the format. This is the only archetype that overrides BRAND.md's caption rule.
- **Top-half headlines are bold sans, not serif.** Mode B headline cards use heavy sans-serif (DM Sans Bold, Cabinet Grotesk Black, Inter Black). Serifs belong to sage-stack and editorial-magazine.

### Color hierarchy under your brand

The source reels use brand-locked emphasis colors (Lindy gold, Krause cyan, Carpathia red). Under your own brand the hierarchy collapses to two:

- **Default emphasis word (most reels):** `--primary-mid`
- **Premium / urgency / proof / money beats only:** `--accent-warm`
- **One color per video.** Choose at the top, set as `--accent` on the reel root, never switch mid-reel.
- **Don't reuse the source-reel creator-coded colors** (Lindy gold, Krause cyan, Carpathia red).

Mode B headline two-tone split: ink (`--anchor-near-black`) + accent (`--primary-mid` default, `--accent-warm` for premium).

### What does NOT translate from the source reels

- **Don't use Lindy gold, Krause cyan, or Carpathia red.** Each is creator-coded.
- **Don't desaturate the speaker.** Naturally-graded face is the entire point of this archetype.
- **Don't add whip-blurs or light-leaks.** Hard cuts only. Whips belong to sage-stack.
- **Don't switch caption colors mid-reel.** One accent per video.
- **Don't use serif headlines.** Mode B is bold sans only.

## 9 · Pre-flight Checklist (run before claiming a video matches this style)

1. ☐ Hard horizontal split at 50% (or 52/55%) with bottom half = continuous speaker
2. ☐ Speaker video has **NO filter** (naturally graded, real environment visible)
3. ☐ Mic visible in every speaker shot
4. ☐ Top half rotates between Mode A (screen rec), Mode B (headline), Mode C (custom dashboard) — at least 2 modes used in any 30s+ reel
5. ☐ Captions sit at **y=65-75%** (mid-torso of speaker), never on the top-half graphic
6. ☐ Caption font is a **heavy sans (≥800 weight)** — Inter Black, Geist Black, SF Pro Display Heavy
7. ☐ Each caption group has at most **one emphasis word** in the brand accent color
8. ☐ The accent color is consistent across the entire reel (no mid-reel color switches)
9. ☐ Accent color matches the dominant top-half brand
10. ☐ Word-level reveal pacing: 0.4-0.8s per word group — captions tick forward, never block-load
11. ☐ Emphasis word has a 0.18s scale-pulse (1.18 → 1.0) on appearance
12. ☐ Top-half transitions are **hard cuts** or 80ms cross-fades — NO whip-blurs, glitches, or light-leaks
13. ☐ Mode B headline cards have the small-caps kicker subtitle above the headline
14. ☐ Mode B headlines use two-color split typography (ink + accent)
15. ☐ Mode C dashboards have a subtle accent-color glow (`drop-shadow` or radial gradient)
16. ☐ Final beat is a full-screen speaker close (top half drops away) with engagement CTA
17. ☐ Pacing: 3-8s per top-half beat for a 30-120s reel
18. ☐ No bouncy/elastic easing — only `none`, `power3.out`, `expo.out`, `power3.in`
19. ☐ Optional grain overlay at ~2.5% opacity for film feel (low priority)
20. ☐ "Would this clip feel at home next to the Krause / Lindy / Carpathia reels?" gut check — if it feels too designed or too cinematic, dial back the motion
